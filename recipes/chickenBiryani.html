Chicken Biryani Recipe

Chicken Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian dish that combines tender chicken, fragrant basmati rice, and a blend of rich spices. This iconic dish is known for its layers of marinated chicken, saffron-infused rice, and caramelized onions, all cooked to perfection in one pot. It’s a complete meal that’s perfect for festive occasions or a hearty family dinner.

The dish has many regional variations, each with its unique blend of spices and cooking methods. From Hyderabadi to Lucknowi styles, Chicken Biryani showcases the rich culinary heritage of the Indian subcontinent. Its bold flavors and tantalizing aroma make it a favorite for food enthusiasts worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 6-8 black peppercorns
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands (soaked in warm milk)
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (for cooking rice and chicken)

Steps

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil in a large pan. Add cumin seeds, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Remove half of the onions and set aside for garnishing.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste to the pan and sauté for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Mix in the chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and biryani masala. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil separates from the mixture.
  7. Stir in yogurt, chopped coriander, and mint leaves. Mix well and cook on low heat until the chicken is tender.
  8. In a separate pot, boil water with salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70-80% done. Drain the rice and set aside.
  9. Layer the cooked chicken mixture at the bottom of a large pot. Spread half of the partially cooked rice over it.
  10. Drizzle saffron milk over the rice and layer the reserved fried onions on top.
  11. Repeat the layers with the remaining chicken mixture and rice. Drizzle more saffron milk and ghee over the final layer.
  12. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse and the rice to fully cook.
  13. Once done, fluff the biryani gently with a fork to mix the layers slightly.
  14. Serve hot, garnished with fresh coriander and mint leaves, alongside raita or salad.
